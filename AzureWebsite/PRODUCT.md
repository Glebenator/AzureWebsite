# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is the project owner, who reads, compares, and experiments with a personal research archive. Public readers may also inspect published notes and source-grounded summaries. Both audiences need to understand what evidence was found, which answer-writing mode was used, and why an answer was not shown.

## Product Purpose

The research viewer makes a private-source Markdown archive readable and queryable without weakening source provenance. Success means answers remain traceable to exact published headings, experimental modes are explicit and reversible, and failure states tell the truth about evidence and policy decisions.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous, candid, editorial. The interface should feel like a well-kept research notebook: calm enough for sustained reading, precise about system state, and honest about uncertainty.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a generic chat bubble, a clinical decision tool, an AI copilot dashboard, or a decorative card-heavy SaaS interface. Do not use safety language as vague legal copy or make experimental output look authoritative.

## Design Principles

- Show provenance at the point of every answer.
- Name system decisions plainly, especially guardrail refusals and missing evidence.
- Keep experimentation visible, reversible, and subordinate to the reading workflow.
- Preserve the editorial research-viewer rhythm instead of introducing chat-product conventions.
- Use progressive disclosure for technical detail while keeping the current mode unmistakable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA behavior. All controls must work by keyboard, expose programmatic labels and state, maintain visible focus, avoid color-only meaning, respect reduced motion, and remain understandable on narrow screens.
