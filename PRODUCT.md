# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Public visitors, technical readers, prospective collaborators, and people exploring the research archive. They need to find, read, and interrogate long-form notes without losing the connection between a summary and its exact supporting passage.

## Product Purpose

AzureWebsite presents Gleb Gladyshevskiy's work and a source-preserving research library. The research assistant should help readers synthesize the library or one article while keeping every answer bounded by the published notes, transparent about uncertainty, and directly navigable back to the relevant headings and references.

## Brand Personality

Precise, editorial, calm. The interface should feel authored and technically rigorous, with restrained language and visible evidence instead of generic AI personality.

## Anti-references

- Floating chat bubbles that compete with reading.
- Generic AI SaaS panels, gradient decoration, glass surfaces, and repeated card grids.
- Opaque answers that hide retrieval or invent links.
- Medical certainty, personalized treatment guidance, or confidence scores presented as truth.
- Interfaces that make search and synthesis look like the same operation.

## Design Principles

1. Put sources before synthesis: every useful answer stays one action away from the exact article passage.
2. Protect the reading flow: Ask is inline and optional, never an overlay or persistent interruption.
3. Separate finding from answering: catalog search locates notes; Ask synthesizes retrieved passages.
4. Make uncertainty useful: insufficient, conflicting, stale, or unavailable evidence gets a clear state.
5. Earn trust through restraint: no arbitrary URLs, hidden model behavior, or decorative AI theatrics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All controls must work by keyboard, preserve visible focus, expose status changes to assistive technology, respect reduced-motion preferences, maintain readable line lengths and contrast, and never rely on color alone. Health-related interactions must use plain language and avoid encouraging users to disclose personal medical information.
